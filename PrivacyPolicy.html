Privacy Policy
Effective Date: April 7, 2026 Last Updated: May 28, 2026

Summit ("the App," "we," "our," or "us") is a fitness coaching application developed by Luke Migas. This Privacy Policy explains what data Summit collects, how it is used and stored, who it is shared with, and what choices you have regarding your data.


The short version: Summit has no advertising SDKs and no analytics trackers. We do not operate any server, sell your data, or use it for advertising. The only services that ever receive your data are Anthropic's Claude API (so the AI coach can generate coaching content), Apple iCloud (so your data syncs across your own Apple devices, if you have iCloud enabled), and Sentry (so we receive anonymized crash reports when the App breaks). Sections 4.1, 4.3, and 4.4 explain each in detail.

1 Data We Collect

1.1 — Apple HealthKit Data

With your explicit permission, Summit reads the following data from Apple HealthKit:

  • Workout data: type, duration, distance, calories, heart rate, cycling power & cadence, running power & speed
  • Heart & recovery metrics: resting heart rate, HRV, VO2 max, blood oxygen (SpO2)
  • Body metrics: body weight, body fat percentage
  • Activity metrics: steps, flights climbed, active & basal calories, distance
  • Sleep data: sleep analysis (duration, stages)
  • Reproductive health data: menstrual cycle tracking data, including cycle start dates, period duration, and cycle phase — used solely to provide cycle-aware coaching context (e.g. adjusting training intensity recommendations based on cycle phase)

Reproductive health data is treated with the same protections as all other HealthKit data: it is stored only on your device, is never used for advertising, and is never sold or shared with third parties. It is sent to Anthropic's Claude API only as part of the core coaching function described in Section 2.1.

Summit reads the HealthKit data listed above with your permission. Summit also writes a limited set of data back to Apple Health by default — specifically, workouts you record or log in Summit (as Apple HealthKit workout sessions, including heart-rate and power samples from any paired Bluetooth device). The write feature is controlled by the "Apple Health Write" toggle in Summit's Settings; you can turn it off at any time, in which case Summit stores workouts locally only and Apple Health will not see them. You can change which categories Summit reads from or writes to Apple Health at any time in iOS Settings → Health → Data Access & Devices → Summit. Summit does not write to any other HealthKit category (no HR independent of workouts, no sleep, no body metrics, no recovery data).

1.2 — Profile Information You Provide

During setup you may voluntarily provide your name, fitness level, training phase, goals, preferred activities, available equipment, coaching style preferences, and personal notes.

1.3 — Workout & Recovery Data You Enter

You may also enter RPE ratings, workout notes, manually logged sessions, and recovery logs (sleep quality, energy ratings, wellness notes).

1.4 — Device Location

Location is used transiently only. Summit accesses your location solely to retrieve local weather conditions for coaching context. It is not stored, not logged, and not transmitted to any server — it is used at the moment of the request and immediately discarded.

1.5 — Claude API Key

If you provide your own Anthropic Claude API key, it is stored exclusively in the iOS Keychain — Apple's hardware-backed encrypted credential store. It is never stored in plaintext and is never transmitted to anyone other than Anthropic's own API endpoint.

2 How We Use Your Data

2.1 — AI-Powered Coaching (Core App Function)

Summit's primary function is AI-generated personalized fitness coaching. To generate coaching responses, Summit sends relevant portions of the following data to Anthropic's Claude API:

  • Your HealthKit health and workout metrics
  • Your profile information (fitness level, goals, preferences)
  • Your workout logs, RPE ratings, and notes
  • Your recovery logs and wellness notes
  • Current weather conditions derived from your location

This is the core function of Summit. Without sending this data to Anthropic's AI, Summit cannot provide coaching. By using Summit's coaching features you explicitly consent to this data transfer.

2.2 — Local Storage

All data is stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. It is used to display your workout history, maintain coaching context across sessions, and personalize your experience over time.

2.3 — What We Do NOT Do With Your Data

  • Use your data for advertising of any kind
  • Sell your data to any third party
  • Use Apple HealthKit data for advertising, marketing, or data mining
  • Share Apple HealthKit data with third parties for advertising or marketing
  • Operate any advertising or behavioral-analytics SDKs (Sentry is included only for crash reporting — see Section 4.3)
  • Profile you for purposes unrelated to fitness coaching

3 Apple HealthKit Compliance

In accordance with Apple's HealthKit guidelines, Summit confirms the following:

  • Summit does not use HealthKit data for advertising purposes
  • Summit does not sell HealthKit data to advertising platforms, data brokers, or information resellers
  • Summit does not share HealthKit data with third parties for advertising or marketing
  • HealthKit data is shared with Anthropic's Claude API solely to provide the App's core fitness coaching functionality
  • HealthKit data is stored only on your device — never on any Summit-operated server
  • Reproductive health and menstrual cycle data is never used for advertising, never sold, and never shared with any third party for any purpose other than generating your personalized coaching content

4 Third-Party Services

4.1 — Anthropic (Claude AI)

Summit sends health metrics, workout data, recovery data, and profile information to Anthropic's Claude API to generate coaching content. Anthropic processes this data under their own privacy policy, available at anthropic.com/privacy.

Summit does not control how Anthropic handles data once received. We encourage you to review Anthropic's privacy policy to understand their data practices.

4.2 — Apple HealthKit

Summit reads HealthKit data based on the permissions you grant. Apple's own privacy policy governs HealthKit. Manage permissions anytime in iOS Settings.

4.3 — Sentry (Crash Reporting)

Summit uses Sentry (sentry.io) for crash reporting and non-fatal error capture. When the App crashes or encounters a recoverable error, Sentry receives a diagnostic report so we can diagnose and fix bugs.

What Sentry receives:

  • Crash stack traces (the code path that failed)
  • Non-identifying device info (iOS version, device model, app version, app environment — debug / TestFlight / production)
  • Non-identifying app state tags (e.g. current training-phase label, streak length) attached to the event for triage
  • Breadcrumb trails — recent app events (button taps, view transitions) preceding the error
  • Lightweight release-health pings (an indicator that the app session started, did not crash, and ended normally)

What Sentry does not receive:

  • Your name, email, Apple ID, or any other personally-identifying information
  • Your IP address (we explicitly disable Sentry's default PII collection)
  • Your HealthKit data, workout content, recovery data, coaching conversations, profile information, or location
  • Your Anthropic API key

Sentry processes diagnostic data under their own privacy policy at sentry.io/privacy. Crash reporting is integral to maintaining App quality; it is not optional in the current version of Summit, and there is no in-app toggle to disable it.

4.4 — Apple iCloud (Cross-Device Sync)

Summit uses Apple iCloud (specifically CloudKit, Apple's cloud-database service for personal data) to sync your Summit data — workout history, training plans, profile, recovery logs, and coaching memory — across your own Apple devices when you are signed in to the same iCloud account on each device.

iCloud data is stored in your personal Apple iCloud account, not on any Summit-operated server. We do not have access to data stored in your iCloud account. Apple's own privacy policy governs iCloud data: apple.com/legal/privacy.

If you do not want Summit data to sync via iCloud, you can disable iCloud for Summit specifically in iOS Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud → Apps Using iCloud → Summit. When iCloud sync is off, Summit stores data only on the local device and your devices will not share data with each other.

4.5 — No Other Third Parties

Beyond Anthropic (AI coaching), Apple (HealthKit and iCloud), and Sentry (crash reporting), Summit does not integrate with any advertising networks, analytics services, social media platforms, or other third-party SDKs.

5 Data Storage & Security

5.1 — Storage

Summit does not operate its own server, cloud database, or remote backend. Your Summit data lives in two places: (1) on your iOS device, in Apple's SwiftData local storage, and (2) in your personal Apple iCloud account, via Apple's CloudKit service, so that your data syncs across your own Apple devices when iCloud is enabled. Apple's iCloud and CloudKit are governed by Apple's own privacy policy. We do not have access to your iCloud-stored data.

Your data leaves your device and iCloud account only in three circumstances:

  • When portions are sent to Anthropic's Claude API to generate coaching content (Section 2.1)
  • When diagnostic / crash reports are sent to Sentry if the App crashes or errors (Section 4.3)
  • When workouts are written to Apple Health, if you have the Apple Health Write feature enabled (Section 1.1)

5.2 — API Key Security

If you provide a Claude API key, it is stored in the iOS Keychain, which uses hardware-backed encryption on supported devices. The key is never stored in plaintext and is used only to authenticate requests to Anthropic.

5.3 — Data in Transit

All data transmitted to third parties (Anthropic, Sentry, Apple iCloud) is sent over HTTPS using TLS-encrypted connections.

6 Data Retention

6.1 — On Your Device

Summit retains your data on your device for as long as the App is installed. Because all storage is local, your data persists until you take action to remove it.

6.2 — Data Sent to Anthropic

Data sent to Anthropic's Claude API is subject to Anthropic's retention policies. See anthropic.com/privacy for details.

7 Your Choices & Rights

7.1 — HealthKit Permissions

Grant or revoke any HealthKit data category at any time via iOS Settings → Health → Data Access & Devices → Summit.

7.2 — Location Permissions

Grant or revoke location access anytime in iOS Settings. Revoking location disables weather-aware coaching; all other features continue to function normally.

7.3 — Clear Coach Memory

Clear the AI coach's conversation memory at any time from within Summit → Settings. This removes all stored coaching context used to personalize ongoing conversations.

7.4 — Reset Your Profile

Reset your profile information at any time from within Summit → Settings.

7.5 — Delete All Data

Delete all Summit data by deleting the App from your device. Uninstalling Summit removes all locally stored data on that device. If you have iCloud sync enabled, your data will also remain in your Apple iCloud account until you sign in to Summit on another device (in which case it restores), disable Summit's access to iCloud in iOS Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud → Apps Using iCloud → Summit, or delete your iCloud account.

7.6 — Disable Apple Health Write

Toggle off "Apple Health Write" in Summit's Settings if you do not want Summit to write workouts to Apple Health. Existing entries Summit previously wrote to Apple Health remain as they are — you can remove them via the Apple Health app.

7.7 — Questions

Contact us at summit.training.app@gmail.com with any questions about your data.

8 Children's Privacy

Summit is intended for users who are 18 years of age or older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under the age of 18. If you are under 18, do not use Summit. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided data to Summit, please contact us at summit.training.app@gmail.com and we will promptly remove their information.
